# Impeccable — ABE Education

This file captures the design context for `abe-site` (abeeducation.edu.au).
Future design work should consult this rather than ask again.

---

## Design Context

### Users

Two primary audiences, both arriving in research mode:

**Owner-builder applicants** — Australian homeowners (typically 30–65) navigating
their state's residential building permit system for the first time. Often not
technical. Often anxious about regulatory exposure: missed deadlines, voided
contracts, the 7y6m statutory warranty period, not understanding what they
"can" and "can't" do under the permit. Decision is regulator-driven, not
brand-driven — they're here because Building Commission NSW (or its
equivalent) told them to be.

**Working tradespeople** — doing CPD or White Card training to maintain
licences. Time-poor, mobile-first, practical mindset. Less anxious than the
homeowner cohort but equally motivated by regulatory pressure rather than
delight. Course completion is a means to an end (licence renewal, site
access).

**Common context across both**:
- Often on phone in low-light/poor-signal conditions (in the garage, on site, at the kitchen table after work)
- Older skew on the homeowner side — assume some users are 50+ on phones with default-large system fonts
- Year-9 reading level — owner builders span all education levels and many haven't dealt with regulators before; jargon must be defined or avoided
- Stressed-buyer cognitive state — calm, practical, plain-English copy beats enthusiasm
- Australian English. Australian dollars. Australian regulators. No drift to American defaults.

**Job-to-be-done**: Confirm I'm in the right place → confirm this course meets
the regulator's requirement → confirm who actually delivers it (the partner
RTO) → confirm price and duration → enrol with confidence that I won't get
caught out by a compliance gap.

### Brand Personality

**Three words: Reassuring · Australian · Practical**

- **Reassuring** — the audience is anxious about regulators and contracts. The
  interface should feel like a competent neighbour walking you through it, not
  a sales funnel. CTAs should feel solid, not urgent. No countdown timers, no
  "limited time", no shouting.
- **Australian** — local idiom over corporate-speak; named NSW/QLD regulators
  written out in full at first mention; AUD prices formatted via
  `Intl.NumberFormat('en-AU')`; dates via `Intl.DateTimeFormat('en-AU')`;
  spellings: organisation, licence (noun), centre, programme. Never
  "comprehensive."
- **Practical** — every section earns its place. If a paragraph doesn't help
  the reader complete the JTBD, cut it. Verification notes are inline, sources
  are dated, and authority signals (developer / reviewer credit, RTO
  partnership disclosure, last-reviewed date) appear without ceremony.

**Emotional goals (in order)**: Confidence → Clarity → Calm. Delight is a
distant fourth — it's a regulator-driven service, not entertainment.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone**: Editorial-meets-utility. Reads like a regulator handbook
crossed with a well-run trade publication — confidence through correctness,
not design theatrics.

**Theme**: Light only. Authoritative reading on phones in daylight. Dark mode
is explicitly off (`color-scheme: light`, `prefersdark: false` in the DaisyUI
theme block) — the audience and viewing context don't need it.

**Reference (canonical)**: The v0.3 NSW Owner Builder mockup (`docs/owner-builder-nsw-mockup.html` style — currently adopted on the live course page). Heritage Maroon CTAs, Cool Off-White section bands, Charcoal text on white surfaces, structured cards for role separation, generous spacing, micro-CTAs at section boundaries.

**Anti-references** (explicit "do not look like"):

1. **Generic SaaS landing page** — no Stripe/Linear/Vercel gradient hero, no
   cyan accents, no abstract tech illustrations, no "Lorem ipsum" testimonials
   from fake-name avatars, no purple-to-blue marketing gradients.
2. **Direct competitor course-marketplaces (e.g. Edway)** — no hard-sell
   patterns: countdown timers, scarcity banners, before/after testimonials,
   urgent red CTAs, "limited spots", "92% of students recommend" badges,
   five-star carousels.

**Brand palette** (committed, hex-locked from the Grey Design System v1.1
— defined in [src/styles/global.css](src/styles/global.css)):

| Role | Hex | OKLCH note |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary** (Heritage Maroon — CTAs, brand accents) | `#800000` | Reserve for CTAs and the brand itself; do not use for body text |
| **Secondary** (Steel — secondary buttons, info callouts) | `#4a5568` | Cooler than the primary; use sparingly |
| **Accent** (Steel Pale — pill / badge backgrounds) | `#e2e8f0` | The understated highlight |
| **Neutral** (Charcoal — body text, dark surfaces, footer) | `#1a1a1a` | Tinted dark, never pure black |
| **Base 100** (page background) | `#ffffff` | Tinted white, never pure |
| **Base 200** (alternating sections, subtle bands) | `#f9fafb` | Cool Off-White |
| **Base 300** (card borders, dividers) | `#e5e7eb` | Cool Border |
| Success | `#059669` | Reserve for confirmations / Eligible badges |
| Warning | `#d97706` | Reserve for the exemption-style alert |
| Error | `#dc2626` | Reserve for prohibited / Excluded states |

The 60-30-10 rule applied: 60% Base 100/200 surface, 30% Charcoal text + Cool
Border, 10% Heritage Maroon. Maroon works *because* it's rare.

**Typography** (committed, via Astro 6 Fonts API in
[astro.config.mjs](astro.config.mjs)):

- **Display (`--font-display`)** — Archivo (700/600/500/400)
- **Body (`--font-sans`)** — DM Sans (400/500/600)
- **Code (`--font-mono`)** — DM Mono (400/500)

> **Tension noted**: the Impeccable skill flags DM Sans / DM Serif / Archivo
> as "reflex fonts" to avoid. They are nevertheless committed in the Grey
> Design System v1.1 brand spec and wired into the build. We respect the
> brand commitment. If a future redesign re-opens font choice, that's the
> moment to revisit — not in passing component work.

**Type scale**: Modular, with at least 1.25× steps and clear contrast. Body
type runs at the larger end of typical (~17–18px) given the older homeowner
audience reading on phones. Line-height ~1.55 on body, tighter on display.
Body text capped at ~65–75ch.

**Spatial / layout**:

- Asymmetric, left-aligned, editorial — never centered for centring's sake
- Generous vertical rhythm between sections (`py-14 lg:py-20`); alternating
  white / Cool Off-White section bands
- Cards used deliberately for role separation and pricing emphasis only —
  not as default container for every block
- Micro-CTAs at section boundaries (Heritage Maroon, dotted underline,
  hover transition) per the v0.3 mockup
- Grid breakpoints favour `auto-fit minmax(...)` over hard breakpoints where
  appropriate

### Design Principles

Five principles that should guide every design decision on this codebase:

1. **Confidence through correctness, not theatrics.** Every claim is dated and
   sourced. Verification notes are inline. The build-time
   `prohibitedClaims` guard exists so visual-only emphasis can never sneak
   past the legal boundary. Visual flourish never comes at the expense of
   regulatory clarity.

2. **Heritage Maroon is rare and load-bearing.** The 10% in the 60-30-10 split.
   Reserve `#800000` for primary CTAs, the brand wordmark, the active nav
   link, and a single brand accent on key surfaces (developer card border,
   pricing card border). Never gradient. Never decorative. If it's used
   everywhere, it's used nowhere.

3. **Plain English at year-9 reading level.** Define jargon at first mention.
   Prefer "you'll need" over "applicants must". Prefer "the cost is $180"
   over "investment". Translate regulatory phrases into outcomes ("you'll
   know how to read building plans" not "CPCCOM2001 — Read and interpret
   plans and specifications" — show both, but lead with the outcome).

4. **The page is a regulator handbook, not a product page.** Authority
   signals (named developer, named reviewer, dated review, partner RTO
   disclosure, source links) appear without ceremony at the natural moment
   they're useful. No floating trust badges. No "as featured in" logos. No
   stat counters that count up on scroll. The reader should feel they could
   cite this page in a Service NSW form without embarrassment.

5. **Zero JavaScript by default; motion is restrained.** Native
   `<details>/<summary>` for accordions and mobile menus. CSS-only hovers and
   transitions. `prefers-reduced-motion` honoured strictly. Decoration is
   typographic and chromatic, not animated. Any future JS island must justify
   its KB cost against a measurable user need.

## Anti-pattern checklist (the AI Slop Test, applied to this brand)

If a design review answer to "AI made this" would be yes, the following are
the most likely tells on **this** project specifically. Reject:

- Maroon-to-cyan gradients on hero text
- Floating maroon glow / pulse effect on the primary CTA
- Stock-photo hard-hat illustrations as section dividers
- Rounded-square icon tiles above every H2
- "Last seat available!" or countdown timers under the price
- Five-star carousels of fake-name testimonials
- Grey side-stripe borders on cards (banned by the skill anyway)
- Gradient text via `background-clip: text` (banned by the skill anyway)
- Centred-everything hero with three feature columns of equal width
- Light/dark theme toggle in the header (this is light-only, intentionally)

## A11y commitments

- **WCAG AA minimum** — Lighthouse Accessibility 100 is the spec target.
  Heritage Maroon `#800000` on white passes AA contrast (8.8:1) for normal
  text. Any future accent colour must be re-tested before adoption.
- **Body type bumped** — defaults run larger than the typical 16px floor to
  accommodate the older homeowner audience on phones in poor light.
- **Reduced motion strict** — `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` is honoured
  in [src/styles/global.css](src/styles/global.css). Card hover-lift and
  transitions are nulled when set. Any future motion must follow the same
  pattern.
- **Semantic HTML first** — `<details>` for accordions and the mobile menu;
  `<dialog>` for any modal that must exist (modals discouraged in general);
  proper heading hierarchy with one `<h1>` per page.
